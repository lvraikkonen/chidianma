# ADR 0001: Lightweight Identity Boundary

Status: `Accepted`

Date: 2026-07-15

## Context

The product uses generated identities, local bearer Tokens, group membership and rotating group
invites. It intentionally has no email/OAuth account. Stage 7B must make this model credible for a
small internal beta without prematurely building a formal account system.

## Decision

- Display name is presentation only, is not unique proof of identity, and must not be treated as an account. Admin is a role of a group membership, not a property of a person or identity.
- Admin, Extension and another device may use one lightweight identity through a short-lived, single-use identity link code generated by any device that still holds a valid Identity Token.
- When all Identity Tokens are lost, the supported path is to create a new identity and rejoin with a group invite. There is no long-lived recovery code or identity merge.
- When a sole admin loses all Tokens, an operator may replace that admin after verifying the known teammate relationship outside the product.
- Suspected device exposure is handled by identity-wide reset of all connections. Stage 7B does not support revoking only one remote device.
- Group membership and role are revalidated on every protected request. Removing a member revokes only that membership, not the lightweight identity or its memberships in other groups.
- Stage 7B does not add email login, OAuth, verified personal identity, formal accounts or account merging.

## Decision boundary

Retain lightweight identity for the first controlled beta after Stage 7B closes legacy paths,
adds public API protections and documents reset/removal semantics. Do not add OAuth, email login or
account merging in 7B.

Identity PII is available to operators for scoped export and anonymization support during the controlled beta. Anonymization revokes active memberships and removes identifying profile data while preserving de-identified historical attribution. The support target is seven days.

Any confirmed identity misuse or authorization bypass immediately pauses cohort expansion. Stage 7D must produce a formal-account ADR if the first cohort requires at least two operator identity recoveries, or if more than 20% of weekly active identities need duplicate-identity or recovery support in any week.

## Consequences

The beta remains low-friction and small in scope, but cannot promise verified personal identity or
cross-device continuity. Stage 7D must measure duplicate identities and recovery/support requests.
